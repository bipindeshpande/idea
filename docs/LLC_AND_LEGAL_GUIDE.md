## ðŸ“Š Launch Without LLC: What to Know

### **Legal Structure: Sole Proprietor**

**What it means:**
- You're operating as an individual
- Business income = personal income
- No legal separation between you and business
- Personal liability for business debts

**Tax implications:**
- Report income on Schedule C (Form 1040)
- Pay self-employment tax (15.3%)
- Can deduct business expenses
- Simpler tax filing

**Liability:**
- Personal assets at risk
- But SaaS has low liability risk (no physical products, no employees initially)

## ðŸš¨ Risks of Launching Without LLC

### **1. Personal Liability**
**Risk:** If someone sues your business, they can go after personal assets

**Reality for SaaS:**
- **Low risk** - You're not selling physical products
- **Low risk** - No employees initially
- **Low risk** - Using Stripe (they handle payment disputes)
- **Medium risk** - If you give bad advice in your AI tool

**Mitigation:**
- Get business insurance ($200-500/year)
- Use clear Terms of Service
- Use clear Privacy Policy
- Disclaim liability in your service

---

### **2. Tax Complexity**
**Risk:** Mixing personal and business finances

**Mitigation:**
- Open separate bank account (even as sole proprietor)
- Track all business expenses
- Use accounting software (QuickBooks, FreshBooks)
- Hire accountant for tax filing ($200-500)

---

### **3. Credibility**
**Risk:** Less professional appearance

**Reality:**
- Most customers don't care
- Many successful startups started as sole proprietors
- You can use a DBA (Doing Business As) name

## ðŸ›ï¸ How to Form an LLC (When Ready)

### **Option 1: DIY (Cheapest - $50-200)**
**Steps:**
1. Choose state (usually your home state)
2. File Articles of Organization
3. Get EIN from IRS (free)
4. Create Operating Agreement (template)
5. File annual reports

**Time:** 2-4 weeks
**Cost:** $50-200 (state fees)
**Difficulty:** Medium
